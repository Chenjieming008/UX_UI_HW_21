Project Context

This project was part of a module challenge I undertook while honing my skills in UX/UI design and coding at the Columbia University bootcamp. It represents not only my ability to create user-centered design solutions but also my commitment to continuous learning and applying new knowledge in practical settings.

Project Reflection

The Department of Labor website redesign allowed me to apply classroom theories to a tangible user experience challenge. This project was an integral component of my portfolio, showcasing my growth as a designer and my readiness to contribute effectively in a professional environment. It was an opportunity to experiment with design principles and techniques in a way that was both guided by experienced instructors and driven by real user data and feedback.

Project Overview

Challenge

The existing Department of Labor website presents a significant challenge for users seeking vital information. The site's complex structure, with its dense menus and convoluted navigation pathways, often leads to frustration and inefficiency. This complexity hinders the website's primary function as a resource for critical labor-related information, from employment laws to benefit details.

Objective

The primary goal of this redesign project is to transform the Department of Labor website into a paragon of user accessibility. By focusing on simplification and intuitive design, the aim is to create a seamless user experience that enables quick access to necessary information without the stress of navigating a labyrinthine digital space.

Approach

Streamlined Menus

Recognizing that many users rely on search capabilities, we invested in advanced search algorithms and filters. These improvements allow for more accurate and relevant search results, drastically cutting down the time users spend hunting for information.

User-Centric Design

To ensure the website caters to its users' needs, we adopted a user-centric design philosophy. This meant creating clear, logical pathways to information and reducing clutter on the webpage. Every design decision, from typography to color scheme, was made with the end-user in mind.

Outcome

The redesigned Department of Labor website stands as a testament to the power of user-friendly design. Post-launch metrics indicate a substantial decrease in user bounce rates and an increase in overall satisfaction. The website now serves not just as a repository of information but as an efficient tool for the public to engage with the Department of Labor's resources.
